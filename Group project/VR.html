What is it?

VR

Virtual Reality sounds just like what it means, to replace reality with something new virtual, computer-generated environment. The idea is to trick your brain into thinking that that's your reality. There are three different types of VR feedback - visual, auditory and haptic, which means what you see, what you hear and what you feel.

VR and AR are two slightly different technologies, so to clarify, virtual reality is replacing your entire reality while augmented reality is just changing the reality a bit, it adds some things with the realworld and oftentimes be in form of on overlay or seeing something on top of a real world object. What makes VR so impressive is the immersion factor.

Headsets

A virtual reality headset is a head-mounted device that provides you with virtual reality. Big brands like Google, HTC, Samsung, Facebook, and Playstation are the ones providing the VR headsets. although VRs are widely used with video games, they are also used in applications, including simulators and trainers. They consist of a stereoscopic head-mounted display (providing separate images for each eye).

VR arcades

VR arcades are becoming more common among the gaming community. They are allowing players to fully experience VR gaming without having to purchase a headset of their own. VR arcades are serving a targeted and growing community.

Educational VR

Just as teachers have different teaching methods, students have different learning methods. Educators have realized that interactive games can be a very effective way for students to absorb the material in the classroom. The possibilities are endless, limited only by the imaginations of content creators.
